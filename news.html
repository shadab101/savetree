Bengaluru: Plans to develop a proposed six-lane flyover between Basaveshwara Circle and Hebbal in Karnataka's state capital have run into trouble following stiff opposition by local residents and city-based activists. With an estimated cost of Rs 2,400 crore, construction of the 6.72 km long road began with the objective of cutting down the travel time of motorists and de-congesting the streets of Bengaluru. Proposed by Bangalore Development Authority (BDA), the idea was to build an elevated flyover along the roads leading to the city's international airport in Devanahalli area. However, following a spate of citywide protests, the project was scrapped. A report suggesting that the project would cost Rs 1,800 crore and would also result in the felling of close to 800 trees led to the outcry.
